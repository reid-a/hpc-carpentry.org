You Belong in The Carpentries!

The Carpentries works to help institutions and individuals spread skills for data analysis, computational thinking, and research software development through building local and global communities of practice.

Our work is made possible by the efforts of our amazing volunteer Instructors, Trainers, Mentors, Maintainers, curriculum advisors, helpers, workshop hosts and organisers, community champions, and member organisations.

Volunteer with us!

Instructors

Instructors teach workshops and are trained in evidence-based pedagogical practices.

Instructor development

The Instructor Development committee helps Instructors continuously improve their teaching practices.

Trainers

Trainers are senior Instructors who train new Instructors both virtually and face-to-face.

Lesson Maintainers

Lesson maintainers work to keep existing lessons up-to-date and improve them, based on community feedback.

Curriculum advisors

Curriculum advisors guide lesson developers and maintainers on curriculum content and strategic direction.

Assessment

The assessment team works to help us understand our impacts from community activities such as workshops.

Champions

Champions work together to share strategies to spread our impact in their own organisations and communities.

Lesson Infrastructure

The infrastructure team works to keep the lesson template and other infrastructure working smoothly..

Regional Coordinators

Regional Coordinators manage workshops, communicate with hosts and instructors, and serve as the front face of The Carpentries in their regions.

Code of Conduct

The Code of Conduct committee works to keep our broader community friendly and welcoming.

About HPC Carpentry

HPC Carpentry is a set of teaching materials designed to help new users take advantage of high-performance computing systems. No prior computational experience is required - these lessons are ideal for either an in-person workshop or independent study. More ›
